Chapter 2 - Coming

OF HIS COMING
Bjaltsarn, the Stormbringer, was lost in a fog. He battled the Orr, and their sorceries, and the shadows of his mind. The world was his enemy, and the Blackhand would merrily smash, and stab, and cleave it apart, if only it meant the end. The end of the Orr, the end of this misery, the very end of the world.
Weeper.
He cuffed an Orr by the neck with his left hand as he smashed his mighty hammer against its jaw. The Orr's lower tusks tearing blood from its upper lip, its jaw shattered by the thunderous blow. Its muscled frame, or what was left of it, crumbling to the earth as the Blackhand stepped past. Past the fury and shadow of this battle, and up to the next.
Did I?
"Not today." Whispered the Blackhand in a voice that was not his own, the rumble of red-thunder in his throat, the rage of red-lightning in his eyes. He cast a wild gaze around, but there were no Orr left to fight. Fled or fallen, it was of no consequence, and so the Blackhand set upon their tents. Roaring unto their camp as a tempest, he flailed and snarled with the fury of a storm. A lone figure hid amidst the camp, a rose-gold silhouette against the fog and fury. Bjaltsarn, the Blackhand, set upon her with murderous intent.
The world was his enemy, and so too were all those in it.
